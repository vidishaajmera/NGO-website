Projects

Teen Tech Tutor

Teen Tech Tutors is where the members help senior citizens learn basic technological skills that not only develops the skill set of the senior citizen and enables them to perform activities more efficiently but also evokes empathy in the members.

Women in STEM Interview Series

In today’s world, where STEM is becoming increasingly prevalent as a means to encourage more girls to pursue STEM career paths or consider it for further education, we have connected and are planning to connect with women who are professionals in STEM.

Workshop with Angel Express

In collaboration with Shespires and Angel Xpress NGO, a two-day workshop was held for students in grades 7 to 10. On the first day, the students were introduced to economics, business, and psychology through basic concepts and interactive sessions, concluding with a positivity activity where they wrote messages on sticky notes. The second day included discussions on famous books like I Am Malala, a build-a-story activity, and learning a pre-prepared dance routine.

Fundraisers

Annual Book Sale Fundraisers

Our annual book sale fundraisers encompass books that are donated by our members and sold at 50% off. Each year we donate the proceeds to a charity/NGO like Nanhi Kali, Project Adhikar, etc.

Jogathon Fundraiser – Jog for a Cause

The HHC collaborated with the Interact Club of Mumbai Jewels to organize a Jogathon fundraiser wherein the 50 NGO kids and 25 members were jogging for 5km. All proceeds were donated to Saaksharta NGO.

Monsoon Biking Fundraiser

The HHC collaborated with the Interact Club of Mumbai Jewels to organize a Bikathon fundraiser wherein the 15 members were bicycling for 10km. All proceeds were donated to the Rotary Club of Mumbai Jewels’ project to help children of house helpers and vendors with supplies like notebooks, stationary and hot meals. 350 of these were distributed to the needy children.

Care for Cancer Fundraiser

In honor of Breast Cancer Awareness Month, the HHC organized a Care for Cancer fundraiser wherein a few of our members curated small handmade items and sold it at an individual level like magnets, bookmarks, bracelets, etc. and had online sessions like housie and Diwali paper flower making, etc. All proceeds raised were donated to the Care for Cancer program by Artscapes. We also conducted an interview with a breast cancer survivor to raise awareness and inspire hope.
